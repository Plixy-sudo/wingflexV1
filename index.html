This aerodynamic capability is not just about comfort; it is about survival. During extreme turbulence, a rigid wing would encounter forces capable of snapping the main spar. A flexible wing, however, dissipates this energy.

Engineers use a concept called "Aeroelasticity" to model these behaviors. It is the interaction between aerodynamic forces, structural elasticity, and inertial forces. In the past, this was a danger zone (leading to flutter), but today it is a design feature.
